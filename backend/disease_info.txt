Atopic Dermatitis:
reason: Genetic predisposition, immune system dysfunction, environmental triggers.
precaution: Moisturize regularly, avoid irritants, manage stress, use prescribed topical medications.
deadly: No, but significantly impacts quality of life.
contagious: No.
symptoms: Itchy, dry, cracked, or inflamed skin.
cure: There is no cure, but symptoms can be managed.

Melanocytic nevus:
reason: Genetic factors, sun exposure.
precaution: Monitor for changes (ABCDEs of melanoma), protect from sun.
deadly: Usually not, but can rarely develop into melanoma.
contagious: No.
symptoms: Moles or skin growths that are usually benign.
cure: Moles are typically benign and don't require a cure.

Melanoma:
reason: UV radiation, genetic predisposition, moles.
precaution: Sun protection, regular skin checks, early detection.
deadly: Yes, if not detected and treated early.
contagious: No.
symptoms: Changes in existing moles, or new moles that are asymmetrical, have irregular borders, varied colors, a diameter greater than 6mm, or are evolving.
cure: Early-stage melanoma is highly curable with surgical excision.

Squamous cell carcinoma:
reason: Cumulative sun exposure, UV damage, HPV, weakened immune system.
precaution: Sun protection, early detection, treatment of precancerous lesions.
deadly: Yes, if left untreated and metastasizes.
contagious: No.
symptoms: Firm, red bump or a flat sore with a scaly crust.
cure: Usually curable with early detection and treatment (e.g., excision).

Tinea Ringworm Candidiasis:
reason: Fungal infection.
precaution: Avoid contact with infected individuals/animals, keep skin dry, use antifungal medications.
deadly: No, but can be persistent and uncomfortable.
contagious: Yes.
symptoms: Ring-shaped rash (ringworm), itching, redness, scaling.
cure: Antifungal medications are effective.

Vascular lesion:
reason: Genetic predisposition, aging, blood vessel abnormalities.
precaution: Usually no precautions needed; treatment for cosmetic reasons or if bothersome.
deadly: No.
contagious: No.
symptoms: Visible red or purple marks on the skin.
cure: Treatment varies depending on the type and severity (e.g., laser therapy).

Benign keratosis:
reason: Aging, sun exposure.
precaution: No specific precautions needed, monitor for changes.
deadly: No.
contagious: No.
symptoms: Waxy, scaly, or slightly raised growths on the skin.
cure: Not usually needed, can be removed for cosmetic reasons.

Actinic keratosis:
reason: Chronic sun exposure, UV damage.
precaution: Strict sun protection, regular skin exams.
deadly: Can develop into squamous cell carcinoma if untreated.
contagious: No.
symptoms: Rough, scaly patches on sun-exposed areas.
cure: Cryotherapy, topical medications, or other procedures.
